Gapminder Bubble Chart (2020)

This iconic visualization reveals how health, wealth, and population intersect across the globe. The bubble size represents each countrys population, making it easy to see that some of the worlds most populous nations (like China and India) have middle-range GDP and life expectancy values, while smaller European nations achieve high life expectancy despite varying economic levels.

Interpretation

The scatterplot reveals a strong positive relationship between GDP per capita and life expectancy in 2020 (correlation coefficient: 0.62). Key observations:

  1. Diminishing Returns: The relationship follows a logarithmic pattern, where increases in GDP have a stronger effect on life expectancy at lower income levels. Beyond approximately $20,000-30,000 GDP per capita, additional wealth yields smaller improvements in life expectancy.

  2. Economic Threshold: Countries with very low GDP per capita (below $5,000) tend to have substantially lower life expectancies (50-65 years), while wealthier nations consistently achieve life expectancies above 75 years.

  3. Health-Wealth Connection: This pattern suggests that economic development enables better healthcare infrastructure, nutrition, sanitation, and living conditions, which are fundamental determinants of population health and longevity.

  4. Variance at Higher Incomes: Among wealthier nations, there is more variation in life expectancy, suggesting that factors beyond incomesuch as healthcare system efficiency, lifestyle, and social policiesplay important roles in determining health outcomes.

In summary, while economic prosperity is strongly associated with longer life expectancy, the relationship is not linear, and countries must invest wisely in health and social systems to translate economic gains into improved population health.
